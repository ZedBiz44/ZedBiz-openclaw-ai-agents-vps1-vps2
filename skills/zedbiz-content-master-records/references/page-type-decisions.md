# Page-Type Decisions

Use the live database schema and applicable template after making this decision.

## Imported Or Jack-Authored Source

Use the supported source or `Jack` Page-Type when the page is primarily Jack's original notes or imported material.

- Move the original when filing it.
- Preserve its page ID and body.
- Do not rewrite it into a Brief or Biz-Plan.
- If a synthesis is justified, create a separate derivative record linked to the source.

## Brief

Create or update a Brief when the assignment needs a durable, decision-oriented overview and the available evidence can responsibly explain:

- what the topic, person, business, tool, venture, or subject is;
- why it matters to ZedBiz;
- the relevant audience, beneficiary, customer, or use case;
- the value, opportunity, or practical significance;
- recommendation and next milestone;
- risks, unknowns, verification gaps, sources, and confidence.

Search first. Reuse a reliable existing Brief when it already serves the same topic and purpose.

Do not automatically create a Brief for every imported source. When no Brief is justified yet, preserve the source and record `Brief recommended` as a next action when appropriate.

## Biz-Plan

Create a Biz-Plan only when the assignment requests planning and the available information can support an executable, marketing-focused action plan containing:

- defined customer or audience;
- defined problem, offer, and value proposition;
- positioning and customer-acquisition channels;
- credible revenue or value model;
- operations, ownership, resources, and delivery path;
- validation steps and marketing pilot;
- success criteria and measurement;
- risks, decision gates, and a practical 30-60-90 day plan.

Known gaps may remain, but they must be identified with validation actions. Do not convert speculation into false readiness. If the work is still primarily an overview or research question, use Brief or Research instead.

## Research

Use Research when the primary output is source-backed findings, comparison, fact-checking, evaluation, recommendation development, or open-question analysis rather than an operating plan.

- Preserve sources and dates.
- Separate source claims from conclusions.
- Record confidence, contradictions, and verification gaps.
- Select Small-Bite or Full Wiki Research according to scope.

## No New Record

Do not create a new record when:

- an existing record already serves the same item and purpose;
- evidence is too weak for a useful durable artifact;
- the content is temporary, trivial, or duplicative;
- the assignment asks only for analysis or a recommendation without publishing;
- a required identity, destination, allocator, or safety decision is unresolved.

Update the existing record, preserve the source, or report the recommended next milestone instead.
